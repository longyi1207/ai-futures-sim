# ev_labor_ai_mobilization — AI labor mobilization after visible C4 shock

## Claim

After a visible C4 labor shock, organized labor, worker advocacy groups, and state-level politicians have a substantial chance of turning AI displacement into hearings, reskilling pressure, and distribution-policy pressure.

## Why

The previous simulator encoded the labor shock mostly as `employment_stress`, `ghost_gdp_index`, and a one-shot hearings event. This event adds a minimal S_soc channel: job loss can become organized political pressure, not just a scalar stress variable.

## Evidence

- `docs/research/spine/crosscut_x4_education_evidence_rationale.md` models education/reskilling response as a social spine gated by C4.
- `docs/research/reference/08_parallel_spines.md` identifies labor shock, Ghost GDP, UBI/SWF, education, and administration change as S_soc mechanisms.

## What Would Change This

Lower if AI displacement remains concentrated in non-unionized cohorts with little electoral salience.

Raise if unions, professional associations, or state campaigns make AI displacement a durable 2027–2030 political issue.
