# ev_alignment_salience_early — early alignment salience before canonical C10

## Claim

Before the canonical C10 public panic, there is a substantial chance of earlier alignment salience once systems pass the superhuman-coder / internal-lab transition. This includes researcher exits, eval papers, controlled lab disclosures, and niche hearings that move transparency norms but do not by themselves force compute pauses.

## Why

Node 4 distinguishes two phenomena that the first simulator version compressed into one event:

- early salience at C6–C8, already visible in 2024–2026 through scheming research, insider exits, and transparency advocacy;
- the full C10 public whistleblower cycle, which requires stronger internal evidence plus a mainstream trigger.

This event models the first phenomenon. It increases public salience and weakly raises the chance of both later internal deception flags and production interpretability halts, without directly opening the extinction chain.

## Evidence

- `docs/research/spine/node4_evidence_rationale.md` §5 separates alignment salience from full Node 4.
- Apollo scheming work and public insider episodes provide the reference class for salience without compute policy.
- The C10 / Trigger E branch remains represented by `ev_c10_internal_concern` and the whistleblower mutex group.

## What Would Change This

Lower if frontier labs or regulators credibly dismiss pre-C10 eval signals for multiple years with no insider/media amplification.

Raise if C6–C8 systems produce repeated public scheming/eval incidents, controlled disclosures, or congressional hearings before a C10-grade whistleblower cycle.
