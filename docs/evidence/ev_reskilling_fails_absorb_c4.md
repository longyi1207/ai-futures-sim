# ev_reskilling_fails_absorb_c4 — reskilling fails to absorb C4 labor shock by 2032

## Claim

Conditional on a visible C4 labor shock and no scaled federal reskilling response, the modal outcome is activity without absorption: pilots, PR, and guidance exist, but displaced cohorts are not re-employed at comparable wages by 2032.

## Why

This ports the S_soc X4 crux into the engine. In the prior simulator, education failure lived in notes while the engine represented it only indirectly through high employment stress. The event gives that failure a named path and lets it push friction outcomes through employment, inequality, autonomy, and `reskilling_absorption`.

## Evidence

- `docs/research/spine/crosscut_x4_education_evidence_rationale.md` sets CX-EDU-RESPONSE at ~0.60.
- The same file gives EV-EDU-FED at ~0.22, represented by `ev_fed_edu_reskilling`; that event locks this failure path.

## What Would Change This

Lower if large-scale, placement-audited programs close the Canaries-style cohort gap and re-employ displaced workers at near-prior wages.

Raise if training programs remain guidance-only or if capability progress outpaces credential pathways.
