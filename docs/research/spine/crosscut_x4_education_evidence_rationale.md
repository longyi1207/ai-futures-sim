### P = 0.60 — CX-EDU-RESPONSE: reskilling fails to absorb C4 shock by 2032

**Claim:** **~60%** education/reskilling policy **does not** close the Canaries employment gap or re-employ displaced junior white-collar cohort at ≥80% prior wage within 18 months by **2032** — scattered pilots + corporate PR, not mass absorption.

**Why:** (1) **Time asymmetry** — METR 50%-horizon doubles ~90 days; credentialed pathway = 6–24 mo + 12–24 mo bill lag. (2) **So-so automation incentive** — firms profit from junior cuts without retraining obligation (Block, Cloudflare). (3) **Federal fiscal headwind** — MASA consolidation proposed, WIOA reauth flat; no **>$10B** step-change. (4) **Corporate programs** optimize pipeline/brand, not Challenger-displaced devs.

**Evidence:**
- [`crosscut_x4_education_evidence_rationale.md`](crosscut_x4_education_evidence_rationale.md) §CX-EDU-RESPONSE — P=0.60, range 0.50–0.70
- [`node1_evidence_rationale.md`](./node1_evidence_rationale.md) — Canaries −16% rel. live; Challenger 101k AI-cited YTD; policy peak ~2027 Q2 (+30% lag)
- [METR Time Horizon 1.1](https://metr.org/blog/2026-1-29-time-horizon-1-1/) — 88.6-day doubling post-2024
- [Canaries paper](https://digitaleconomy.stanford.edu/publication/canaries-in-the-coal-mine-six-facts-about-the-recent-employment-effects-of-artificial-intelligence/) — Fact 4: 22–25 cohort −16% rel.
- [`../reference/05_crux_registry.md`](../reference/05_crux_registry.md) — CX-EDU-RESPONSE stub

**Analogue:** Post-2008 TAA — trade-adjustment training authorized but **underfunded** relative to shock scale; displacement normalized without pathway closure.

**Would update if:** Canaries gap closes to **<5% rel.** for 4+ quarters **and** federal training spend ≥0.3% GDP/yr sustained **and** median displaced-worker re-employment wage ≥85% prior within 18 mo.

**Conf:** **M**
