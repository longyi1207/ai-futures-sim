## Definition

**Capability C:** Not capability-driven — **institutional** node. Fires when **non-US actors and multilateral forums** materially shape frontier AI pace: Brussels-effect enforcement, sovereign-AI blocs, summit diplomacy, and treaty attempts (binding or not).

**Falsifiable claim (by ~2030):** Global governance **fragments** into US-led acceleration, EU deployment regulation, China/Global-South access narrative, and Gulf compute — **without** a verified training-limit treaty. **Exception tails:** EU GPAI enforcement binds US labs; empty-shell treaty creates false security; Taiwan hot war.

**Fixes US-centric blind spot:** Nodes 1–6 weight Congress, CA, GAAIA. Node 9 makes **Paris 2025 / India 2026 / EU AI Office / UAE third pole** first-class — they often **move faster** than US federal law on **deployment** duties while **blocking** pause frames.

## Japan — METI, Hiroshima AI Process, chip alliance

**Governance posture:** Innovation-first, **no AI-specific fines**. AI Promotion Act fully effective **Sep 2025**; AI Basic Plan (Dec 2025) + AI Strategy Headquarters (PM-chaired). METI/MIC **AI Guidelines for Business v1.2** (Mar 2026) = soft law; Japan AI Safety Institute = eval, not certification.

**Multilateral:** Japan **hosts and leads** Hiroshima AI Process (HAIP) follow-on — Friends Group **66 countries + 38 orgs** (Mar 2026 Tokyo meeting); **Action Plan 2026** shifts from principles → voluntary reporting + Global South capacity (JICA). HAIP Reporting Framework announced at **Paris 2025**. Japan is the **bridge** between G7 guardrails and Global South access narrative — **not** a pause coalition.

**Chip / compute alliance:** METI **23-category** SME export controls (Jul 2023); Nov 2025 order adds FPLD modules. **US–Japan Genesis Mission** (Jan 2026): **$1B** (DOE + MEXT/METI) for next-gen computing + AI science. **MATCH Act** pressure (Apr 2026 US bill): 150-day ultimatum to align controls or lose US market access for Japanese equipment makers — forces Japan into **US-led compute chokepoint** role, not independent treaty broker.

| Outcome | P(by 2030) | Conf |
|---------|------------|------|
| HAIP remains voluntary guardrails (no verification) | **0.85** | M |
| Japan domestic AI Act stays light-touch (no EU-style fines) | **0.75** | M |
| Japan aligns chip controls with US if MATCH passes | **0.55** | M |
| Genesis / Rapidus / TSMC Kumamoto ↑ allied frontier FLOPs | **0.60** | M |
| Japan joins verified training-limit treaty | **0.04** | L |

**Node 9 read:** Japan **↑** guardrails diplomacy (M1) and **↑** allied compute; **↓** probability of independent Asia-Pacific pause bloc. Correlates Node 7 (compute concentration) and Node 3 (export-control escalation).

## South Korea — AI Basic Act & sovereign AI industrial stack

**Regulatory:** Framework Act on AI Development and Establishment of Trust (**AI Basic Act**) in force **22 Jan 2026** — first comprehensive APAC AI statute. Extraterritorial; high-impact AI + generative AI transparency; National AI Committee (President); AI Safety Research Institute. **Grace period ≥1 year** (2026): fines deferred except serious harm — MSIT "minimum regulation."

**Sovereign AI (industrial):** APEC 2025 — Nvidia **260,000 Blackwell GPUs** across Samsung (50k), SK (50k), Hyundai (50k), Naver (60k), government (50k) → **300k+** installed capacity. Samsung SDS consortium building **National AI Computing Center** (Haenam; **2.5–2.9T won**, 15k AI chips by 2028). Lee administration "Third Way" — sovereign value chain, open-source national foundation model (WBL project), presidential Chief AI Adviser (ex-NAVER).

## India — deep dive: sovereign stack & enforcement reality

### Sovereign infrastructure (Yotta, BHASHINI, IndiaAI Mission)

- **IndiaAI Mission:** ₹10,000+ crore — compute, models, talent, apps, energy (five-layer stack per MeitY).
- **Yotta + BHASHINI:** Full migration to **Shakti Cloud / GCC** (Feb 2026); Maha Kumbh 2025 population-scale test (11+ languages, 200+ TiB, 3.5B files); claimed 40% perf ↑, 20–30% cost ↓ vs hyperscaler.
- **New Delhi Frontier AI Commitments:** Voluntary — global + Indian frontier labs; multilingual eval, deployment insights for policy.

### New Delhi Declaration — enforcement reality

**91 signatories** (US + China both signed); seven "chakra" pillars — access, sovereignty, workforce, **no training moratorium**. MediaNama / Economic Diplomacy / DFRLab consensus: **voluntary, non-binding**; no mandatory reporting, funding, or fines. **Does not reference** UN Global Dialogue or Scientific Panel — parallel governance track.

### Domestic enforcement (what actually bites)

| Instrument | Status 2025–26 | AI-relevant teeth |
|------------|----------------|-------------------|
| **DPDP Act 2023 + Rules 2025** | Rules notified Nov 2025; 18-month phased compliance; Board operational | Training-data consent, breach notification, up to ₹250 crore penalties — **full bite ~May 2027** |
| **IT Rules 2021 + Amendment 2026** | In force | Deepfake/synthetic labeling, intermediary takedown — **operational now** |
| **IndiaAI Governance Guidelines** (Feb 2026) | Soft law / principle-based | No standalone AI Act; sectoral gaps under review |
| **New Delhi Declaration** | Signed Feb 2026 | **Zero** enforcement mechanism |

| Outcome | P(by 2030) | Conf |

**Node 9 read:** India is **simultaneously** the strongest **anti-freeze diplomatic** actor and a **moderate domestic enforcement** tail (DPDP + IT Rules). Do **not** conflate summit signatures with pause capacity.
